## Part A – HackerRank (Coding Practice)

**Platform:** HackerRank (hackerrank.com)

**Challenge Completed:** Solve Me First (Algorithms – Warmup)

**What I did:**
- Made an account using my college email
- Went to the Algorithms section, then Warmup
- Did the "Solve Me First" challenge — basically you read two integers and print their sum
- Submitted it and got full marks

**What I learned:**
HackerRank sorts problems by domain and difficulty, which is pretty helpful. It also runs test cases on your code automatically and shows which ones passed and which didn't. That part was a bit different from just writing code in a normal editor — it kind of pushes you to write cleaner, input-output based code.

## Part B – Google Forms (Collaboration Tool)

**Platform:** Google Forms + Google Sheets (forms.google.com)

**What I built:**
A 5-question **Digital Literacy Awareness Quiz** — something that could actually be used to test basic digital literacy knowledge among first-year students.

**Questions included:**
1. You receive an urgent email from "Google Security" saying your account will be deleted unless you click a link to verify your password — what's the first thing you should check? *(Multiple Choice)*
2. Which of the following are considered best practices for managing your online passwords? *(Checkbox — Select all that apply)*
3. On a scale of 1 to 5, how confident are you in your ability to tell apart a "Deepfake" video from a real recording of a public figure? *(Linear Scale)*
4. In your own words, briefly explain why "Incognito Mode" or "Private Browsing" does NOT make you completely anonymous on the internet. *(Short Answer)*
5. When an app asks for permission to access your Contacts, Location, and Microphone during installation, what is the most digitally literate action to take? *(Multiple Choice)*

**Google Form Link:** https://docs.google.com/forms/d/e/1FAIpQLSfuciJfD6a7uZ1ltNB9VYjtZ_ja0jPeh1V474XpoJnHVCL3DQ/viewform?usp=publish-editor

**How it helps academically:**
Google Forms is actually really useful — you can collect survey responses, run peer quizzes, or gather group feedback without any hassle. It automatically saves all responses into a Google Sheet, so analysing the data is straightforward and you don't need to code anything.
