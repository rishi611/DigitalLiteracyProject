# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students
### Task 4 – Email Etiquette | CSE0001 Digital Literacy

---

## DO's ✅

1. **Make your profile worth visiting** – Your profile photo and bio are usually the first things anyone sees. Use something appropriate and make sure your bio actually reflects where you're headed academically or professionally — recruiters and internship coordinators do check these things.

2. **Run the "screenshot test" before posting** – Before hitting post, picture that content sitting in front of your professor, a parent, or someone interviewing you. If that makes you uncomfortable, it probably shouldn't go up.

3. **Revisit your privacy settings regularly** – Platforms update their settings all the time, and defaults are usually set to share more, not less. Go in every few months and make sure only the right people can see what you're sharing.

4. **Credit the original creator** – If you're sharing or reposting someone else's work — a photo, an article, a quote — take two seconds to tag or mention them. It's both respectful and the right thing to do.

5. **Engage without turning it into a fight** – You're allowed to disagree with people online. Just do it in a way that's actually worth reading. Thoughtful disagreement is fine; going after someone personally isn't.

6. **Treat LinkedIn and GitHub like a living resume** – These platforms lose their value if they're outdated. Whenever you finish a project or pick up a new skill, update them. You never know who's looking.

7. **Use the report button — that's what it's there for** – If you come across harassment, hate speech, or accounts that feel off, report them. Scrolling past it and hoping someone else handles it isn't really an option.

---

## DON'Ts ❌

1. **Your personal details are not public content** – Phone number, home address, college ID, Aadhaar, bank details — none of this belongs on any social media platform, even in a "private" post. Once it's out, you can't take it back.

2. **Stranger requests deserve skepticism** – If someone you don't know sends a request or slides into your DMs asking for something — money, personal info, or pushing a suspicious link — ignore and block. No explanation needed.

3. **Sharing unverified news makes you part of the problem** – Misinformation spreads fast, and "I didn't know it was fake" doesn't really hold up. Take a minute to verify before you share, especially anything that sounds dramatic or urgent.

4. **Cyberbullying doesn't fix itself if you stay quiet** – Whether it's directed at you or someone else, staying silent isn't neutral. Report it, support whoever's being targeted, and don't contribute by engaging with the bully.

5. **Assume everything you post is permanent and public** – Deleting a post doesn't mean it's gone. Screenshots exist. Private settings can change. Write and post accordingly.

6. **No legitimate service will ever ask for your PIN or OTP** – Not a bank, not a payment app, not "customer support" on Instagram. If anyone asks for these, it's a scam. Full stop.

7. **DMs are not the right channel for academic or professional matters** – If something actually matters — a deadline, a complaint, an opportunity — send an email. Social media messages are easy to miss, easy to misread, and hard to follow up on officially.
