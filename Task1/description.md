# Task 1 – Infographic Description
### CSE0001 Digital Literacy | VIT Bhopal University

---

## Tool Used
**Canva** (canva.com) – It's basically a free browser-based design tool. Pretty easy to use, no installations needed.

## What the Infographic Shows

The infographic is called **"Your Guide to Digital Success"** and I designed it as a one-page visual for first-year B.Tech students. It covers three main things:

1. **Safe Internet Practices** – Things like creating strong, unique passwords for every account, always verifying sources before sharing anything, protecting personal details, thinking before clicking any link, and reporting suspicious activity to trusted adults.

2. **Digital Literacy Essentials** – A short intro to what digital literacy actually means — basically the ability to find, evaluate, and use digital information effectively. The idea is that it helps students navigate the online world with more confidence.

3. **Useful Digital Tools for Students** – A quick look at tools that actually help in college: Google Docs for collaborative writing, Notion for organising notes and projects, Canva for making visual presentations, and Grammarly for improving writing. The focus was on tools that improve both productivity and creativity.

## Design Choices

I went with a clean, professional colour scheme to keep things easy to read. I tried to keep each section short and to the point — long paragraphs don't really work in an infographic format. The layout was kept simple so it doesn't feel overwhelming at first glance.

## One Thing Found Difficult

Honestly, the hardest part was figuring out how much to put in without making it look too crowded. I ended up limiting each section to a few short points, and that helped keep the layout clean and readable.
